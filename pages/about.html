About echoIA & the Shape Catalogs Portal

echoIA is a community effort to build a unified pipeline for Intrinsic Alignment (IA) analyses. The first step is simple but powerful: collect publicly available galaxy shape catalogs in one place, with clean links and minimal, comparable metadata so everyone can see whatâ€™s out there at a glance.

What is echoIA?

Intrinsic Alignments (IA) are correlations in galaxy orientations caused by large-scale tidal fields. Unlike gravitational lensing (a line-of-sight shear from intervening matter), IA is an in-situ effect tied to galaxy formation and environment. Getting IA right is essential for current and upcoming cosmology surveys to meet their precision goals.

echoIA brings together observers, theorists, and survey teams to coordinate IA measurements and modeling, with a shared vocabulary and interoperable tools that make results easier to compare and combine.

Why start with shape catalogs?

A shape catalog provides per-galaxy shape measurements (e.g., ellipticities/shear) plus basic context like redshifts and quality flags. Catalogs are the raw ingredients for IA analyses. By indexing them with consistent top-level fields and stable links (often DOIs on Zenodo or institutional repositories), we make discovery fast and comparisons fair.

We do not host datasets; each catalog remains under its own license and citation. We only point you there with clear metadata.

What this portal provides

  • Curated index of public galaxy shape catalogs with stable links and licensing notes.
  • Comparable metadata across releases so you can quickly scan scope and applicability.
  • Community submissions to keep the index fresh and correct.

Details of the exact fields we track live on the Catalogs and Submit pages.

Roadmap toward a unified IA pipeline

  1. Collect public shape catalogs with minimal, consistent metadata (this portal).
  2. Harmonize schema & flags to streamline cross-survey use.
  3. Provide reference notebooks/containers that load and validate multiple catalogs.
  4. Establish evaluation benchmarks and best practices for IA systematics.
